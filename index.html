Our Lawyers

John W. Dickie

John W. Dickie specializes in Property Tax Assessment law, Residential Rent Control, Landlord and Tenant Law for landlords, and government relations. With decades of experience, John has been pivotal in numerous key cases, offering indispensable guidance and advocacy for landlords and property managers.

PRACTICE AREAS

John has extensive experience in property tax assessment, residential rent control, landlord and tenant law, and government relations. He has represented a wide range of clients, from small property owners to large residential landlords.

PROPERTY TAX ASSESSMENT

John has practiced Municipal Property Tax Assessment law since 1992, publishing several influential articles and representing major clients in policy matters and property tax reforms.

RESIDENTIAL RENT CONTROL

With a practice dating back to 1982, John has been a leading figure in residential rent control law, acting as an expert witness and co-authoring a legal textbook on the subject.

LANDLORD & TENANT LAW

John has been instrumental in representing landlords' interests in landlord and tenant law, ensuring fair legal processes and outcomes for his clients.

GOVERNMENT RELATIONS

John is deeply involved in government relations, advocating for landlord rights and influencing policy changes. He holds leadership roles in various landlord associations.

EDUCATION

  • Bachelor of Arts (First Class Honours Economics), Carleton University, 1977
  • Bachelor of Laws (LL.B.), McGill University, 1981

PROFESSIONAL MEMBERSHIPS

  • Eastern Ontario Landlord Organization (EOLO)
  • Canadian Property Tax Association (CPTA)
  • County of Carleton Law Association (CCLA)
  • Building Owners & Managers Association of Ottawa (BOMA Ottawa)
  • Canadian Federation of Apartment Associations (CFAA)
  • Federation of Rental-housing Providers of Ontario (FRPO)

TEACHING & SPEAKING ENGAGEMENTS

John has taught courses at Carleton University and the University of Ottawa, and has instructed at the Bar Admission Course of the Law Society of Upper Canada. He frequently presents at industry events.

PERSONAL INTERESTS

John enjoys running, reading, playing bridge, and traveling. He has extensively explored England, Scotland, and all ten provinces of Canada, as well as several countries in Europe. He is happy to share travel tips and sightseeing ideas.

S. David Lyman

David Lyman has been practicing residential rent control, landlord and tenant law, municipal property tax assessment law, and related litigation alongside John Dickie since December 1999. Their partnership was officially formed in November 2003.

PRACTICE AREAS

David's expertise spans across residential rent control, landlord and tenant law, municipal property tax assessment law, and government relations. He has consistently delivered exceptional legal services to his clients.

RESIDENTIAL TENANCY LAW

As the Vice-President of the Eastern Ontario Landlord Organization, David is a trusted advocate for landlords in Ottawa. Since 1999, he has successfully handled over 70 rent review applications, helping landlords optimize rents on tens of thousands of units.

PROPERTY TAX MATTERS

David has been deeply involved in property tax matters since 1999. His work includes reviewing assessments, appealing assessments, negotiating with assessors, analyzing tax allocations, and managing capping and clawback calculations.

COMMERCIAL TENANCY LAW

David represents landlords, property managers, and certain tenants in commercial leasing matters. His expertise includes lease negotiations, lease interpretation disputes, and tax recovery disputes.

GOVERNMENT RELATIONS

David collaborates with John Dickie to represent multi-residential, commercial, and non-profit taxpayers in government relations concerning rent control, landlord and tenant law, property tax exemption claims, vacancy rebate rules, and other significant matters.

EDUCATION

  • Bar Admission Course, 1999, Called February 2000
  • LL.B., University of Toronto, 1998
  • B.A. in Political Science, York University, 1995 (Graduate with Merit)

PROFESSIONAL MEMBERSHIPS

  • Eastern Ontario Landlord Organization (EOLO)
  • Canadian Property Tax Association (CPTA)
  • County of Carleton Law Association (CCLA)
  • Building Owners & Managers Association of Ottawa (BOMA Ottawa)

PERSONAL INTERESTS

David is a devoted father and husband who strives to balance his professional and personal life. He maintains an active lifestyle, participating in various individual and team sports. Additionally, David has been a co-chair of the Legal Division of the UJA since 2004, demonstrating his commitment to community involvement.

PUBLISHED WORKS

Co-author of "Working With The Residential Tenancies Act," a comprehensive legal textbook. David has also contributed numerous articles and papers on property tax and rent control.

TEACHING & SPEAKING ENGAGEMENTS

David frequently presents at industry events and has authored material for various real estate organizations. His contributions to legal education and public speaking are well-recognized in the field.
